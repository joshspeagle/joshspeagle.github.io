About Me

I am a National Science Foundation (NSF) Graduate Research Fellow in the Department of Astronomy at Harvard University working with Daniel Eisenstein and Charlie Conroy.

My research broadly focuses on trying to extract as much information as possible from large extragalactic surveys in ''reasonable'' amounts of time. This involves developing complex statistical models to conduct rigorous Bayesian inference and subsequently leveraging machine learning techniques to scale them up to large datasets. I am especially interested in the ''dregs of science'' regime where lots of data are available but are both noisy and plagued by large (possibly unknown) systematics. These datasets not only offer up a wide variety of interesting data modeling challenges but also frequently contain useful science results that otherwise would remain buried and forgotten.

I am currently involved in the COSMOS, Euclid, HSC, and SDSS-IV MaNGA collaborations.
